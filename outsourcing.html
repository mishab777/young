What is Accounts payable outsourcing & Services we Provide

Accounts payable (AP) outsourcing is a business practice where a company delegates some or all of its accounts payable processes to a third-party service provider. This outsourcing arrangement involves the external management of various AP functions, including invoice processing, vendor management, payment processing, reconciliation, reporting, and other related tasks. Here's a detailed breakdown of accounts payable outsourcing:

Invoice Processing :

AP outsourcing providers handle the receipt, review, coding, and processing of invoices received from vendors. This includes data entry, validation of invoice details against purchase orders or contracts, and routing invoices for approval within the organization's workflow.

Vendor Management:

AP outsourcing involves managing relationships with vendors and suppliers on behalf of the client company. This includes setting up vendor accounts, maintaining vendor databases, responding to vendor inquiries, and resolving payment discrepancies or disputes.

Payment Processing:

Once invoices are approved, AP outsourcing providers facilitate the payment process. This may involve issuing payments via various methods, such as checks, electronic funds transfers (EFT), automated clearing house (ACH) payments, or virtual credit cards. Providers ensure payments are made accurately, securely, and in compliance with payment terms and deadlines.

Reconciliation :

AP outsourcing includes reconciling payments made with invoices and vendor statements to ensure accuracy and completeness. Providers match payments to corresponding invoices, identify discrepancies or errors, and address any outstanding issues to maintain accurate financial records.

Reporting and Analysis:

AP outsourcing providers generate reports and analysis to provide insights into AP performance, trends, and metrics. This includes tracking key performance indicators (KPIs) such as invoice processing time, payment accuracy, aging of payables, and cash flow projections. Reports help identify areas for improvement and inform decision-making.

Compliance and Control :

AP outsourcing providers ensure compliance with internal policies, industry regulations, and statutory requirements related to accounts payable processes. This includes implementing controls, security measures, and audit trails to mitigate risks associated with fraud, errors, and non-compliance.

Technology and Automation:

AP outsourcing leverages advanced technologies, automation tools, and software platforms to streamline AP processes and improve efficiency. This includes electronic invoice processing, workflow automation, data extraction, and integration with accounting systems for seamless data transfer.

Scalability and Flexibility:

AP outsourcing offers scalability to accommodate changes in transaction volumes, business growth, or seasonal demands. Providers can adjust resources and capacity to meet client needs quickly and efficiently, ensuring continuity of service without disruptions.

Process of Accounts Payable

  • Step 1 : Receipt of Invoices
    •Invoices are received from vendors for goods or services rendered.
    • Invoices may be received via mail, email, electronic data interchange (EDI), or supplier portals.
  • Step 2 : Review and Approval
    • Invoices are reviewed for accuracy, completeness, and compliance with purchase orders or contracts.
    • Appropriate approvals are obtained from authorized personnel, such as department heads or managers, based on predetermined approval workflows.
  • Step 3 : Coding and Data Entry
    • Invoices are coded with relevant account codes, cost centers, and project codes for proper allocation and tracking.
    • Invoice details, including vendor information, invoice number, invoice date, amount, and line item details, are entered into the accounting system.
  • Step 4 : Matching Invoices
    • Invoices are matched with corresponding purchase orders (POs) to verify the accuracy of quantities, prices, and terms.
    • Three-way matching involves comparing the invoice, PO, and goods receipt to ensure consistency and prevent discrepancies.
  • Step 5 : Payment Autherization
    • Invoices are matched with corresponding purchase orders (POs) to verify the accuracy of quantities, prices, and terms.
    • Three-way matching involves comparing the invoice, PO, and goods receipt to ensure consistency and prevent discrepancies.
  • Step 6 : Payment Processing
    • Payments to vendors are processed using approved payment methods, such as checks, electronic funds transfers (EFT), automated clearing house (ACH) payments, or virtual credit cards.
    • Payment files are generated and transmitted to banks or payment platforms for execution.
  • Step 7 : Reconciliation and Posting
    • Payments made are reconciled with invoices and vendor statements to ensure accuracy and completeness.
    • Accounts payable transactions are posted to the general ledger and other relevant accounts in the accounting system.
  • Step 8 : Vendor Communication
    • Communication with vendors is maintained throughout the AP process to address inquiries, resolve disputes, and provide payment status updates.
    • Vendor relationships are managed effectively to ensure timely and accurate payments and maintain positive rapport.
  • Step 9 : Reportinga and Analysis
    • Reports and analysis are generated to track AP performance, aging of payables, cash flow projections, and other key metrics.
    • Analysis of AP data helps identify trends, outliers, and opportunities for process improvement and optimization.
  • Step 10 : Compliance and Audit
    • AP procedures are designed to ensure compliance with internal policies, industry regulations, and statutory requirements.
    • Regular audits of AP processes, controls, and documentation are conducted to verify accuracy, integrity, and adherence to standards.
