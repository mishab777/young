Registration Process for Corporate Tax in the UAE

In accordance with Federal Decree Law 47 issued by the FTA, all taxable individuals, including Free Zone Persons, are required to register for Corporate Tax and obtain a unique Registration Number. Even those classified as Exempted Persons are urged by the Federal Tax Authority to register for Corporate Tax.

Taxable Persons need to submit Corporate Tax returns within 9 months after the end of a specific period. This deadline typically coincides with the payment of all Corporate Taxes owed for that Tax Period.

In cases specified as exempted by the Minister, Taxable Persons must register for Corporate Tax with the Federal Tax Authority within a timeline set by the authority.

The Federal Tax Authority mandates that Taxable Persons or Independent Partnerships register for Corporate Tax and acquire a Tax Registration Number promptly upon meeting the criteria for being a Taxable Person.

Upon successful corporate tax registration, individuals are subject to a standard rate of Corporate Tax of 9% on taxable income exceeding AED 375,000, with no tax applied on income up to AED 375,000.

Exemptions from UAE Corporate Tax

While the Federal Tax Authority requires Exempted Persons to register for corporate tax, exempted businesses are not liable to pay corporate tax at a rate of 0%.

Certain categories are exempt from Corporate Tax. Some exemptions include:

Employee salaries are not impacted by Corporate Tax. However, individuals earning income from freelancing activities are subject to company tax

Corporate Tax does not apply to dividends, capital gains, or other income from personal investments

Real estate investments are exempt if the investor does not hold a business license

Intra-group transactions or reorganizations are not affected by Corporate Tax

Foreign investors are not taxed on income from dividends, gains, royalties, and similar investment returns unless derived from business activities

If you own a business in the UAE, check if your entity is exempt from corporate tax.
